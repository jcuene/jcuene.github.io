This past weekend I headed out for a long-overdue day hike in the Colorado Rockies. The trail started just outside of town and wound its way up through a dense stand of aspen before opening onto a ridgeline with views in every direction.

## The Trail

The first few miles were a gradual climb through the trees, the kind of quiet that only comes when there's snow on the ground and no wind. By mile three the trail steepened, switchbacking up a rocky face until it crested the ridge.

It's the kind of view that makes the climb feel worth it every time.

## Getting Out There

If you're looking to do this trail, give yourself a full day and bring more water than you think you need. The elevation will remind you it's in charge.
